Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information

Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

1. Introduction

The HSRP Online Portal (transportinfo.online), operated under the guidelines of the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH), Government of India, is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of all users. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, store, and safeguard your information.

2. Information We Collect

When you use this portal, we may collect the following information:

  • Personal Details: Full name (as per RC book), mobile number, email address
  • Vehicle Information: Registration number, vehicle type, chassis number
  • Location Data: State/Union Territory of registration
  • Service Details: Type of HSRP service requested (new, replacement, retain, transfer)
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, device information, and access timestamps for security and analytics
3. How We Use Your Information

Your data is used exclusively for:

  • Processing and tracking your HSRP application
  • Verifying vehicle details against the National Vahan Database
  • Communicating application status updates via SMS or email
  • Coordinating HSRP plate manufacturing and dealer installation appointments
  • Improving portal performance, security, and user experience
  • Complying with legal requirements and government directives
4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. Your data may be shared with:

  • Authorized HSRP manufacturers and dealers for plate production and fitting
  • State/UT Regional Transport Offices (RTOs) for verification purposes
  • Law enforcement agencies when required by law or court order
  • National Informatics Centre (NIC) for technical support and hosting
5. Data Security

We employ industry-standard security measures to protect your data, including:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmission
  • Secure government-grade servers hosted by NIC
  • Access controls and audit logging
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
6. Data Retention

Application data is retained as per the record-keeping requirements of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, and applicable state regulations. Technical logs are retained for a period of 12 months for security and audit purposes.

7. Your Rights

As per the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data held about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Withdraw consent for data processing (subject to legal obligations)
  • Lodge a grievance regarding data handling
8. Cookies

This portal may use essential cookies to maintain session information and improve user experience. No advertising or tracking cookies are used. You may disable cookies through your browser settings, though this may affect certain features.

9. Contact

For privacy-related inquiries, data access requests, or grievances, please contact us through the online form or write to the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways at morth.nic.in.
